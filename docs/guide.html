PixelPainting

Design and 3D-print your own cross-stitch bookmarks — no experience needed.
iskonssss.github.io/pixelpainting

PixelPainting turns a little pixel drawing into a real, printable bookmark: a soft plastic “canvas” mesh with raised, embroidery-style X stitches in your colors. You draw, the app writes the printer’s instructions (a “G-code” file), and a Bambu Lab printer does the stitching. A bookmark takes about 15–30 minutes to print and a few grams of material.

1

Open the app — make it yours

Visit the link above on your phone or computer. On a phone, tap Share → Add to Home Screen and it installs like a normal app (works offline too).

2

Start from a template

Tap 🎨 Templates and pick from 38 designs — flowers, hearts, night sky, rainbows… or start Blank. Everything stays editable afterwards.

3

Paint your design

Each square becomes one stitch. ✏️ paints, 🧽 erases, 🪣 fills an area, 💧 picks up a color from the canvas. One finger draws; two fingers pinch to zoom. Mistake? ↩ Undo.

4

Choose your colors

Tap a color swatch to swap it — pick from ready-made shades or mix your own. Each color = one filament you’ll load during the print, so 2–4 colors keeps it easy.

5

Generate & check

In ⚙️ Settings choose your printer model, then tap Generate G-code. You’ll see a preview plus exactly how much filament each color needs and the print time. Tap Download or Share to save the file.

6

Print it

Copy the file onto the printer’s SD card (or send it over Wi-Fi with the included helper — see the website’s guide). On the printer screen: Files → your file → Print.

⏸ The printer will pause — that’s the magic, not a problem!

The bookmark prints in stages: first the cream canvas mesh, then the stitches, one color at a time. Before each color the printer parks and waits. On the printer’s screen open the filament menu, tap Unload, feed in the next color and tap Load (let it push material through until the new color runs clean), then press Resume. The app tells you the color order before you start.

🧺 What you’ll need: a Bambu Lab printer • PLA filament — one spool for the canvas (cream or white) plus your stitch colors • an SD card or Wi-Fi • about half an hour

🎛 Use PLA filamentThe app’s standard settings are made for PLA — the most common, easiest material. Any brand works.
👀 Watch the first minutesThe first layer matters most. If it’s not sticking, stop, clean the plate, and try again.
🪶 Peel gentlyThe mesh is thin and flexible like fabric. Wait for the bed to cool, then peel from a corner.
💾 Nothing is lostDesigns save automatically in the app, and you can export/import them as files to share with friends.
